# Month-End Close Triage Checklist

## User

Controllers and finance operations teams investigating anomalies during close.

## Triage goal

Identify which journals, reconciliations, or business units deserve immediate review before books are finalized.

## Checklist

1. Rank anomalies by materiality and likelihood of late adjustment.
2. Separate recurring timing noise from new abnormal movement.
3. Check whether reconciliation breaks cluster around one entity, account, or business unit.
4. Confirm whether the anomaly would materially change close timing or reported results.
5. Assign a named owner for any investigation that survives first-pass review.

## Escalate immediately when

- a high-materiality account spikes without a matching business explanation
- reconciliations break across multiple linked accounts
- late manual adjustments are recurring from the same source team
- the anomaly can delay signoff or executive reporting

## Decision output

The controller brief should leave the room with:

- the top anomalies to review now
- the owner for each follow-up
- whether close timing is at risk
- whether a reserve or reporting adjustment is likely
